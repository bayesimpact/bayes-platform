**Analytics** shows how your agents are being used. It's a read-only dashboard of
**conversation metrics** for your workspace — how many conversations happen, how
engaged users are, and how sessions break down by category — that you can filter by
**agent** and by **date range**.

You open it from **Analytics** in the left sidebar of **Studio**. The dashboard
covers the whole **workspace**; each conversation agent also has its own Analytics
view, reached from the agent's page.

> **Optional feature — ask to have it enabled.** Analytics is turned on per
> workspace. If you don't see **Analytics** in the Studio sidebar, the feature isn't
> enabled for your workspace yet — contact the Bayes team to request it.

## What the dashboard shows

The page has a **filter row** at the top right and three charts:

- **Conversations per day** — the number of conversational sessions started each
  day.
- **Avg. user questions per session** — the average number of user messages per
  session, per day.
- **Sessions per day by category** — a stacked bar chart splitting daily session
  volume by **conversation category**.

Each chart shows a headline number and a bar chart over the selected period. The
charts read the sessions your agents have had, so a brand-new workspace with no
conversations yet will show empty or zero data.

## Step by step

### 1. Open Analytics

In **Studio**, open the left sidebar and select **Analytics**. The dashboard opens
for your whole workspace.

### 2. Read the filter row

At the top right you'll find two controls: an **agent filter** and a **Date range**
picker. They apply to every chart below.

### 3. Filter by agent

Use the agent dropdown to focus on a single agent, or keep **All agents** to see the
whole workspace combined.

### 4. Choose the period

Open the **Date range** picker and pick a period (it defaults to the last 7 days).
All charts update to that range.

### 5. Conversations per day

The **Conversations per day** chart shows how many conversational sessions started
each day, with **Total conversations** as the headline number for the period.

### 6. Avg. user questions per session

The **Avg. user questions per session** chart shows the average number of user
messages per session — a measure of how much back-and-forth each conversation
involves.

### 7. Sessions per day by category

The **Sessions per day by category** chart stacks each day's sessions by
conversation category, with a color-coded **legend**. Sessions without a category
appear as **Uncategorized**. With **All agents** selected, categories are combined
across every agent.

## Tips

- Start with **All agents** for the big picture, then filter to one agent to spot
  which agents drive the most activity.
- Compare periods by changing the **Date range** — for example this week vs. last
  week.
- The **by category** chart is most useful once your conversation agents use
  categories; otherwise most sessions fall under **Uncategorized**.
- Each **conversation agent** also has its own Analytics view (from the agent's
  page) if you want to drill into a single agent.

## Troubleshooting

- **I don't see Analytics in the sidebar** — Analytics is an optional feature; it
  may not be enabled for your workspace. Ask your workspace **Owner** or an **Admin**
  if it's missing.
- **The charts are empty** — there may be no conversations in the selected period.
  Widen the **Date range**, or check that the selected agent has had sessions.
- **Everything is Uncategorized** — categories come from your conversation agents'
  configuration; sessions are only split out once agents assign categories.
- **The dates look English** — chart date labels are currently shown in a fixed
  English format regardless of the interface language.
